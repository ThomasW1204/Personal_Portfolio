Real News

AI-powered aggregator utilizing multi-agent pipelines and RAG for news summaries.

Description

Real News is a news dashboard designed to cut through the noise of modern media. Instead of forcing users to wade through long walls of text, Real News uses AI to aggregate, group, and summarize global events into concise, digestible briefs.

Working in a team of four, I was responsible for the AI integration and backend architecture. The system ingests live data via a NewsAPI, which is then processed through a custom-built multi-agent pipeline:

  • Dynamic Clustering: A specialized AI agent analyzes incoming articles and groups them by topic in real-time, ensuring that related stories are consolidated.
  • Specialized Summarization: Once grouped, three distinct agents generate unique perspectives for the user:
    • Generic Summary: A standard, professional overview of the event.
    • Explain like I'm five: A simplified breakdown of complex topics.
    • The Five W's: A structured summary focusing on the Who, What, When, Where, and Why.

    Beyond static summaries, I implemented a context-aware chatbot for every article. Using a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) approach, the bot is grounded strictly in the context of the specific news article. I "locked down" the system to prevent hallucinations and ensure the AI remains unbiased, even when prompted with leading questions.

    To manage operational costs and prevent API abuse, I engineered a tiered rate-limiting system. Anonymous users are identified via IP hashes to provide a limited number of calls, while registered users receive a higher quota.

    This project utilized Typescript, Javascript, Python, HTML, CSS, and SQL
